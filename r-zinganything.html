Cookbook Chat: Estee Kafra's Shavuos Ideas

Estee is the author of 3 best selling cookbooks: Spice it Right, Cooking with Color, and her recent release Cooking Inspired. Together with her partner Dassi she runs KosherScoop.com, a website with thousands of recipes, and all the "latest and greatest in kosher cooking". Follow them on Facebook, Pinterest and Instagram.


How do you make Shavuos special for your family?


I have B"H some cute little ones at home that love to do anything that involves messy and gooey icing and cake. Last year we baked cupcakes and the kids got to decorate them. They each made a "Har Sinai" with icing, fondant, candies, marshmallows, and lollipops. It was a big mess, but they were so proud of their creations.


What food do they look forward to?


The truth is that in our house we have to always be trying new things, so we don't really get that attached to recipes, unless it's for macaroni and cheese. The guys like lemon chicken, the girls like challah and dips... one meat dish that I do make often that is a huge hit is Maple Roast. n the dairy department, they like crispy roasted potatoes with sour cream, and I am a fan of anything that has goat cheese on it... love these... Goat Cheese Crostini with Carmelized Fruit.


People entertain over Yom-Tov. Any good tips and/or recipes that can be made ahead? What will satisfy a crowd?


I believe that a good challah to start the meal with is always the best. On yom tov we can warm it up, so that the crust is crunchy and the inside is soft and warm. Lately I always have garlic dip (recipe in the new book "Cooking Inspired") and I always make or buy others like this one: Eggplant and Garlic Dip that keep in the fridge, and we never get tired of. One meal you can serve the eggplant as a dip, and another meal serve it with cubes of seared sea bass for a wonderful appetizer.


Can you share with us your favourite cheesecake recipe?


My favourite is a true, authentic cheesecake, without too many toppings or fillings. A plain whipped cream cheese base, with eggs, sugar, and vanilla is my favourite. I got a recipe from my friend Raizy Werner a long time ago, and I have made that one many times: New York Cheesecake. I often like to change up the cookies I make the crust with though. Oberlander's lemon cookies give a great flavour, I am a huge fan of the gingersnaps from Shasha breads, and sometimes I use the crust from my Pesach recipe which is filberts, butter, and some sugar.


What tool do you find useful to help you prepare for Yom-Tov?


Since I put lemon or orange zest into everything I possibly can, my Microplane zester is a very good friend.


Can you give us some ideas on decorating your table?


I am a huge fan of bud vases, as they have an elegant look about them, but are easy to put together, and I can do them on my own. Here is a picture of the ones I did for Pesach (see above). As well, I like to put leaves and flowers in the chandelier for Shavuos, as it gives the room a festive look.

Best Brownie Cheesecake


Ingredients:


Bottom:

  • 4 eggs
  • 1 1/4 cup sugar
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • Dash of salt
  • 1 cup flour
  • 1 cup chopped pecans
  • 1/2 teaspoon almond extract
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 6 oz. baking chocolate melted with 1 tablespoon butter

Top:

  • 2.5 containers whipped cream cheese
  • 1 stick butter (minus 1 tablespoon)
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 2 eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 2 tablespoons flour

Directions:


Preheat oven to 350°. Grease or line a 9x13 pan.


For bottom: Mix all ingredients except pecans. Set aside 1/2 cup batter. Add pecans to main batter. Bake for 10 minutes.


For top: Cream cream cheese; add ingredients one at a time. Pour over brownie. Marbelize with reserved batter.


Bake for 45-60 minutes. Cool and cut into squares or rectangles.


Serve frosty.



Coconut Orange Cupcakes


I made these beautiful cupcakes in Wilton pink blossom cups, and you can use the Wilton scallop scissors to make the flower decoration. It looks spectacular for Shavuos, and is really easy to do! This recipe stays very fresh at room temperature for a couple of days.


Ingredients:


  • 2 1/3 cups flour
  • 1 ½ teaspoon baking powder
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup coconut
  • 2 teaspoons orange zest
  • 1 stick margarine, softened
  • 6 ounces Tofutti cream cheese
  • 4 extra large eggs
  • 1 ½ cups sugar

Glaze:

  • 1 cup icing sugar
  • 2 tablespoons orange juice

Decoration:

  • 4 Wilton Sugar Sheets

Preheat the oven to 350ºF. Lay out 24 blossom cups on cookie sheets.


Make the Cupcakes:

Combine the flour, baking powder, salt, coconut and orange zest. Beat the margarine and tofutti cream cheese in an electric mixer until smooth. Add the sugar and mix until fluffy. With the mixer running, beat in the eggs one at a time, mixing well after each addition. With the mixer running at low speed, add the flour mixture slowly. Scrape well.Divide the batter between the 24 blossom cups. Bake for about 30 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted in the center comes out clean. Cool completely.


Decorate the Cake:

In a small bowl, whisk the icing sugar with the orange juice until smooth. Spread on top of the cupcakes. (If the icing is too thick, add some more orange juice).
Cut 5/8 wide strips along the length of each sugar sheet. Use the scallop border insert to cut a scalloped edge on each strip.


Position the strips on the iced cupcakes in a spiral shape, attaching the end of the strips together with a little icing. (You will need two strips per cupcake).


Press gently into the icing to secure.



Dairy Mushroom Turnovers


Ingredients:


Pastry:

  • 8 oz. softened whipped cream cheese
  • 1 1/2 cups flour
  • 1/2 cup cold butter, cut into half inch cubes

Directions:


Place all ingredients into a bowl and, with a for,, mix and mash until dough begins to form. Do not overwork the dough. Press into a disk shape and refrigerate until ready to roll out.


Filling:

  • 3 tablespoons butter
  • 1/2 pound mushrooms, minced
  • 1 large onion, minced
  • 1/4 teaspoon thyme
  • 1/4 cup sour cream
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 2 tablespoons flour

Directions:


In a 10in skillet, over medium heat, cook the mushrooms and onions in hot butter until tender, stirring occasionally. Stir in sour cream, salt, thyme, and flour. Set aside.


Preheat oven to 450ºF. Divide the dough into two parts, and roll out each half on a well-floured surface, to a twelve by twelve inch square. Place one rolled-out half on a well-floured perogie maker. Spoon the filling into the dough lined pockets. Cover with the other half of the dough. Press the top layer of dough, gently. Roll a rolling pin firmly over the perogie maker. Turn the perogie maker over, and tap gently on its back to release the turnovers. Reroll the scraps, and repeat process, this time covering only half the area of the perogie maker. Continue to do this until the dough is used up, covering a smaller area of the perogie maker each time.


Brush with slightly beaten egg.


Bake 12-15 minutes, or until golden.


You can freeze these turnovers unbaked, then pop them right in the oven - just bake a little longer.


Makes approximately 24 turnovers.
