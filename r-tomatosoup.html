Recipe: Tomato Vegetable Soup & Sour Cream Biscuits


Tomato Vegetable Soup


What could be better than a hot vegetable soup on a chilly winter day? I served this with sour cream biscuits, (recipe below), for the ultimate in comfort food.


I used the Alligator onion chopper, (see October issue), to dice my vegetables uniformly small. The Zyliss soft skin peeler is perfect for the tomatoes.


Ingredients:


  • 3 leeks, white part only
  • 2 small potatoes, peeled
  • 1 onion
  • 2 stalks celery
  • 2 medium zucchini
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 4 tablespoons water
  • 7 – 8 cups water
  • 1 tablespoon chicken soup mix
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1 cup frozen sweet peas
  • 6 ripe tomatoes, peeled
  • 5 cloves garlic
  • 6 cubes of frozen basil, or 3 teaspoons dried basil
  • Salt
  • Freshly ground pepper

Directions:


Dice the leeks, potatoes, onion, celery and zucchini.


In a large stockpot, combine 2 tablespoons olive oil with the water. Add the vegetables, and sauté over medium heat until the water evaporates, about 5-10 minutes.


Add the water, chicken soup mix and the bay leaf. Bring to a boil. Lower heat to a gentle simmer, and cook for about 20 minutes. Add the frozen peas, and cook for another 10 minutes.


In the meantime, puree the tomatoes, garlic, basil and 1 tablespoon olive oil in a food processor fitted with a metal blade.


Stir the puree into the cooked soup. Season to taste with salt and pepper.


Serves 10.


Sour Cream Biscuits

Ingredients:


  • 2 cups flour
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1 tablespoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/3 cup shortening
  • 1/2 cup milk
  • 3/4 cup sour cream

Directions:


Heat oven to 450°.


Combine flour, sugar, baking powder and salt; blend well. Using a pastry blender, cut in the shortening until the mixture is crumbly. Add the milk and sour cream, stirring just until moistened.


Pat the dough down to 1/2 inch thickness. Cut out with a 2-3 inch round cutter, making sure to lift the cutter straight up, without twisting. Place on ungreased cookie sheet.


Bake for 10-12 minutes or until golden brown. Serve warm.


Yield: 12-18 buiscuits


Tip: Peeling Garlic? Stay Clean!

Customer Lorraine B. gave me this hint on peeling garlic. Take 2 small stainless steel bowls that are the same size. Place the whole garlic or separate unpeeled cloves in one bowl. Put the two bowls rim to rim, and shake, shake, shake. All the peels will come off the garlic without getting your hands dirty!
