Cookbook Chat: Summertime Cooking with Daniella Silver

Daniella Silver is the author of the fresh new cookbook, The Silver Platter: Simple to Spectacular, geared towards food lovers of all ages looking for new and healthy twists on classic dishes for any occasion. Partnering with cookbook guru Norene Gilletz, Daniella has made a big splash in the culinary world and she sat down with Kitchen Art to share her ideas on summertime cooking.


In your opinion, what defines summer cooking?


Summer cooking is all about being relaxed. When I’m preparing for a summer dinner party, I envision a big outdoor table with family and friends, lots of cold salads being passed around, and of course, barbecue. The food is more casual, and thanks to longer, warmer nights, the evenings usually move at a slower pace than in the winter months. That’s why, for me at least, summer menus typically involve lighter, snackier-type foods. Summer is defined by lazy, low-key comfort, and summer cooking should reflect that. So prep some bite-sized appetizers, and keep them coming all night!


What is the most summery recipe in The Silver Platter?


Well, the truth is there are a lot of them. Summertime invokes a craving for fresh, burst-in-your-mouth flavors, and that happens to be my favorite kind of cooking. I guess you could say that I make summertime dishes all year round! But if I had to choose one, I’d say it’s my Quinoa with Hearts of Palm, Cherry Tomatoes & Avocado. It has all of the true summer necessities: bright in color, fresh in flavor, tastes terrific, quick to prepare, and casual to serve. This medley is a great compliment to barbecued anything.


What is your favorite kitchen gadget?


My favorite gadget is the lemon squeezer - the one that you put straight into the lemon. It's so simple to use!


What advice would you give to readers on how to invoke that fresh summer flavor in their cooking?


ADD LEMON! Bright, light, fragrant—just thinking of a lemon brings freshness to mind! I always have a bag of lemons on hand in the summer, and I use them on literally everything. Just one squeeze of that sunshiney citrus brightens the flavors of salads, soups, grilled meats and even desserts. If you have extra lemons hanging around, slice them up with a few oranges and toss into a water pitcher. Now you’ve got a quick and refreshing drink for the dinner table.


Recipe: Quinoa with Hearts of Palm, Cherry Tomatoes & Avocado (The Silver Platter)

This quintessential medley is bright in color, fresh in flavor, and tastes terrific. Hearts of palm add a silky yet firm texture to this simple side.


Ingredients:


Salad:

  • 3 cups lightly salted water
  • 1 1/2 cups quinoa, rinsed and drained
  • 1/2 cup diced red onion
  • 1 can (14 oz/400 g) sliced hearts of palm, well-drained
  • 2 cups cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1 1/2 cups chopped fresh spinach
  • 1 ripe avocado

Dressing:

  • 1/3 cup extra virgin olive oil
  • 1/3 cup lemon juice (preferably fresh)
  • 2 Tbsp honey
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced (about 1 tsp)
  • 1 tsp kosher salt
  • freshly ground black pepper

Directions:


Bring water to a boil in a medium saucepan over high heat. Add quinoa; reduce heat. Simmer, covered, for 15 minutes or until tender. Remove from heat; let stand, covered, for 5 minutes. Flu! quinoa with a fork. Transfer to a large serving bowl; let cool.


Add onion, hearts of palm, tomatoes, and spinach to quinoa.


Dressing: Combine ingredients for dressing in a glass jar; seal tightly, and shake well.


Shortly before serving, peel, pit, and dice avocado. Add avocado and dressing to quinoa mixture; toss gently to combine. Adjust seasonings to taste. Serve chilled or at room temperature.


Norene's Notes:


Nutritional tip: Quinoa provides life-sustaining nutrients, including all 8 essential amino acids, so it is a complete protein. It is gluten-free, making it an excellent alternative for those who are allergic to wheat.


Use quinoa as a replacement for rice, couscous, bulgur, or wheat berries in salads and side dishes.
